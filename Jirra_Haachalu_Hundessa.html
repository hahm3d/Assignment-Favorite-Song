Jirra by Haachalu Hundessa

Jirra! (We are still alive!) was released in October 2017, during the height of the Oromo protest movement. Haachalu emphasizes the resilience of the Oromo community, highlighting their resistance through specific geographical references. By mentioning locations pivotal to the movement, he illustrates how various parts of Oromia united in pursuit of liberation.

Waa'ee Keenyaa

Haachalu Hundessa's second studio album "Waa'ee Keenyaa" was released in 2013. It features a collection of songs that reflect his deep connection to Oromo culture and his role as a voice for the Oromo people. Jirra is a top single from the album. The album's title translates to "Land of Ours” in English, and it is known for its powerful lyrics addressing social justice, identity, and the struggles faced by the Oromo community. Haachalu Hundessa's music, including his album "Waa'ee Keenyaa," became a powerful symbol of Oromo resistance and resilience. Haachalu's music, including this album, played a significant role in mobilizing and inspiring the Oromo youth during his lifetime.
